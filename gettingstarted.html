  2. Getting started

Depending on what kind of woodworking you would like to take up as a hobby, such as making furniture, carving, scrolling, sculpting, turning, do it yourself fix-its around the home or restoration work, there are two ways to get started. One is to go in for a fully equipped shop. The second, and the better way is to start small and accumulate the tools you need, gradually. This is also a less expensive way to begin, since equipping a workshop can cost you thousands of dollars. Also, there are chances that you might end up adding tools you hardly use. Another thing is that one almost always buys a tool that has more than one use. For example, you can cut dados with a table saw or a router. Curves can be accomplished with a saber saw, a band saw or a scroll saw.

  • Table saw
  • Cordless drill
  • Sander
  • Square
  • Bar clamps
  • Miter saw

  • Ensure that you read instruction manuals related to each tool and equipment you acquire.
  • Check each machine before you operate it.
  • Wear eye protection when necessary and gloves to guard your hands from wood splinters, cuts from blades and saws and finish removers.
  • Your electric equipment must be properly maintained and in good working order.
